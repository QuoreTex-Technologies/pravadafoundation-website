Who We Are

Integrity. Impact. Innovation.

Pravada Foundation is a grassroots non-profit organization committed to addressing the most pressing social challenges of our time. Founded on the principles of truth and service, we work hand-in-hand with local communities to build lasting solutions.

Our holistic approach ensures that every project we undertake—from building schools to implementing clean water systems—is sustainable, scalable, and community-driven.

Our Inspiration

The foundation of our work lies in the lifelong dedication of our founders to social reform and community service.

Visionary Founder

Late Shri Vyankatesh Vishnu Gole

A distinguished social reformer from Pune, Shri Gole's legacy is rooted in the pursuit of education and social justice. His grandfather, M. S. Gole, was a key associate of legendary figures like Lokmanya Tilak and co-founded the Deccan Education Society in 1885.

Inspired by Mahatma Gandhi, Shri Gole joined the freedom movement at 14, even carrying messages to imprisoned fighters. He founded 'Pravada' in Shaniwar Wada—a youth group championing education, hygiene, and women's rights.

Later serving as a professor of history, he dedicated his life to fostering an enlightened generation, a mission we carry forward today.

Mother of the Community

Late Smt. Lata Ambadas Wadwalkar

Known affectionately as "Aai" (mother), Smt. Lataji devoted five decades to the welfare of underprivileged children and vulnerable women. She served with distinction as the President of Bhagini Nivedita Pratishthan and was a pillar of the Andhra Mahila Sabha.

Her pioneering work included establishing crèches in villages like Manikonda and Gandepet, empowering daily-wage working women by providing safe, nurturing environments for their children.

She also took stewardship of the Radhakrishnan Orphanage, ensuring every child received not just meals, but the warmth of a family.

"Her work remains a testament to the transformative power of compassion in action, inspiring generations in social service."

Why Revitalise Pravada

"In a world deluged by irrelevant information, clarity is power" — Yuval Noah Harari

In the first two decades of the 21st century, humans have made extraordinary progress. The industrial revolution and globalization have helped shape a new modern society but opened many doors for the exploitation of the poor, weak, and marginalized. Similarly, digitalization has fostered the most interconnected society in human history, rapidly advancing towards AI, machine learning, and virtual realities.

At the same time, this achievement carries the horror of creating a 'useless class' where most jobs are taken over by algorithms. This widens the socio-economic gap, deepening the crevasse of inequality in opportunities and standard of living.

We at Pravada aim to bridge this gap and create a level playing field for all.

  3. 3. Empowerment and Protection of Women and Children

  6. 6. Addressing Social Evils and Promoting Social Justice.
  7. 7. Sports and Holistic Personality Development
  8. 8. Democratizing Web 3.0 and Emerging Technologies
  9. 9. Self-Defense Training and Mental Wellness Support.
  10. 10. Policy Advocacy and Grassroots Research for Inclusive Growth
